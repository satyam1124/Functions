Ch 3: Running Gear

⏱ 25 min📚 8 topics● INTERMEDIATE

The running gear converts piston reciprocating motion into crankshaft rotation. Components: crankshaft (semi-built/fully-built), connecting rod, crosshead (2-stroke), piston with rod and rings, stuffing box.


In MAN B&W ME engines, the piston rod passes through a stuffing box separating scavenge space from crankcase, allowing different lubricants and preventing contamination.

🚢 Example: MAN B&W 6S50ME-C — crankshaft ~120 tonnes. Deflections checked at 16,000 hr overhaul.
Semi-built crankshaft: Shrink-fit assembly — most common in large 2-stroke engines.
Crosshead: Absorbs side thrust, prevents cylinder liner wear. Only in 2-stroke crosshead engines.
Connecting rod: Forged steel, transmits force from crosshead to crankpin with white metal bearings.
Piston cooling: Bore cooling or jet cooling. Coolant: system oil or separate circuit.
Deflection readings: 5 positions per unit. Excess → bearing misalignment → realign.
