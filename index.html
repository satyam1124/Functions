Chapter 3: Running Gear

⏱ 25 min📚 8 topics● Intermediate

The running gear converts piston reciprocating motion into crankshaft rotary motion. Components include the crankshaft (semi-built or fully-built), connecting rod, crosshead (2-stroke only), piston with rod and rings, and stuffing box.


In MAN B&W ME series engines, the piston rod passes through a stuffing box separating scavenge space from crankcase. The crosshead absorbs side thrust while guide shoes maintain alignment.

🚢 Real Example: MAN B&W 6S50ME-C on a bulk carrier — crankshaft weighs ~120 tonnes. Deflection readings taken at 16,000 hr overhaul to check alignment.
Semi-built crankshaft uses shrink-fit assembly — most common in large marine 2-stroke engines.
Connecting rod transmits force from crosshead to crankpin. Forged steel with white metal bearing shells at bottom end.
Crosshead absorbs side thrust preventing liner wear. Only found in 2-stroke slow-speed crosshead engines.
Piston cooling: Bore cooling or jet/cocktail shaker. Coolant: system oil or separate cooling circuit.
Crankshaft deflection measured at 5 positions to detect bearing misalignment. Excess = realignment required.
